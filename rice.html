Retinopathy of Prematurity Screening Device

International Summer Experience in Engineering Design

Nairobi, Kenya | Jun 2025 - Jul 2025

For my first overseas engineering project, I traveled to Nairobi, Kenya, to address the critical lack of accessible Retinopathy of Prematurity (ROP) screening tools in resource-constrained clinics. After visiting six hospitals and identifying that the reliance on specialists caused dangerous delays in diagnosis, my goal was to design a low-cost device that enabled nurses to capture diagnostic images independently. I spearheaded the mechanical design and rapid prototyping, utilizing 3D-printed PLA to engineer an adjustable phone-based imaging mount capable of linear translation for precise optical alignment. I integrated a dimensionally verified neck support based on neonatal anthropometry and designed the optical housing to theoretically accommodate a standard 20D lens geometry. While my teammates developed image-qualification software to facilitate remote diagnosis, I delivered a functional physical prototype that we demonstrated at Kenyatta University, where the project’s software component ultimately won first place in the 2025 Invention Education Design Challenge.

Adaptive Assist Device for Fiber Artists

Oshman Engineering Design Kitchen

Houston, TX | Sep 2024 - Dec 2024

Our goal was to help a fiber artist who had injured her hand continue spinning yarn independently. Because spinning normally requires two hands, we learned there were no existing assistive tools designed for one-handed fiber artists. Through early conversations with our client, we established key design criteria focused on safety, portability, ease of control, and compatibility with a range of fiber types. During the development process, we observed her spinning workflow, identified the specific hand motions involved, and looked outside the field for mechanical analogies. I focused on mechanical design and prototyping, taking inspiration from one-handed fishing mechanisms to create a structure that supports twisting, gripping, and controlled drafting. Much of my work centered on translating these motions into simple mechanical features that could be operated with a single hand. As a result, we created a portable assistive device that allowed our client to continue spinning fiber into yarn using only one hand. The prototype demonstrated that thoughtful mechanical support can enable users to return to meaningful hobbies after injury, and highlighted how engineering can directly improve quality of life through personalized design.

Custom-Made Hammer

Industrial Processing Lab

Houston, TX | Jan 2025 - May 2025

As part of my Industrial Processing Lab course, I learned machining by fabricating a custom hammer from raw steel, aluminum, copper, and plastic. Robo-Cat

Rice Robotics Club R&D

Houston, TX | Sep 2025 - Present

As part of the Rice Robotics Club R&D team, we’re developing a robotic cat that mimics feline jumping through a multi-linkage leg system. Our goal is to understand how biological motion can be recreated using lightweight mechanisms and compact actuation. I’ve been primarily responsible for the hip-joint assembly, integrating the layered gear system, bearings, and internal wiring to drive the linkage motion while keeping mass low. Much of my work focuses on packaging components like actuators and gears within a small chassis, ensuring smooth rotation, reduced inertia, and reliable transmission during jump cycles.

Wind Turbine Generator

Rice Wind Club

Houston, TX | Jan 2024 - Dec 2024

In this project, I focused on the generator subsystem of the wind turbine, experimenting with coil configurations, magnet types, and magnet counts to maximize power output. I evaluated different wiring arrangements and tested how coil geometry and magnet spacing affected induced voltage. Based on these results, I contributed to the design of the generator housing and integrated the generator with the main turbine assembly so it mechanically mates with the pitch system and rotating shaft the rest of the team developed. This work gave me hands-on experience in electromagnetic design, prototyping, and component integration within a larger mechanical system.
