About Me

Hello! My name is Sherry Choi and I am a student at Rice University ('27) with a Major in Mechanical Engineering and a Minor in Engineering Design. My interests lie in the intersection of Product Design and Biomedical Design. I am passionate about designing devices around the user, ensuring the consumer's point of view is central to the engineering process.

In total, I have 2.5 years of research and internship experience. I started in the MedTech sector as a Robot Development Intern at UTHealth. From there, I pivoted to research to better understand the materials that define the future of product interaction. Currently, I am studying self-healing polymers as application materials for future durable products, and analyzing serpentine conductive traces to advance the mechanical design of bioresorbable bioelectronics. While exploring these interests, I participated in two major engineering design projects where I interacted directly with clients to build user-centric solutions: a Retinopathy of Prematurity (ROP) Screening Device for clinics in Kenya and an Adaptive Assist Device for local fiber artists. In both instances, I collaborated with end-users to design products that specifically addressed their physical environments and accessibility needs.

I am super excited to see where my career journey takes me next!
