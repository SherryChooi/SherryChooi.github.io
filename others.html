Beyond Engineering


I believe that the best engineers are lifelong learners who actively step outside their comfort zones. My journey at Rice has been defined by a drive to explore the unknown, whether that means traveling to Kenya to understand global health challenges, stepping into the corporate world to explore infrastructure at HDR, or picking up an epee to coach the Fencing team. This page is a collection of those pivotal moments. It captures my passion for leadership, my dedication to community, and my ongoing exploration of how Human-Centered Design can bridge the gap between technical innovation and real-world impact.



From Factories to the Operating Room to the Nature

Nairobi, Kenya | Jun 2025 - Jul 2025

This experience was truly mind-opening, taking me far beyond the textbook definitions of engineering. I explored Nairobi’s infrastructure, visiting manufacturing factories to see what materials and fabrication methods were actually accessible locally. However, nothing could have prepared me for the reality of the clinic. The most remarkable experience was standing just a meter away from a C-section surgery in progress. Outside of the hospitals and factories, I also had the chance to escape to the Maasai Mara. Seeing the wildlife and the vast, untouched nature was absolutely breathtaking. Career Exploration in Infrastructure & Systems

Externship with HDR Inc.

San Francisco, CA | Dec 2024 & Mar 2025

I believe that to build effective mechanical components, you have to understand the massive systems they inhabit. Over my winter and spring breaks, I completed an on-site externship with HDR’s Wastewater Department, visiting the West County Wastewater District and the San Mateo Wastewater Treatment Plant. Stepping out of the classroom and onto these industrial sites was a lesson in scale. I observed firsthand how mechanical systems—pumps, valves, and filtration units—integrate with civil infrastructure to serve entire cities. Participating in site walkthroughs and design reviews broadened my perspective on cross-disciplinary engineering, reinforcing my ability to think in systems and clarifying my drive to develop engineering solutions that address fundamental human needs.

Rice Hong Kong Student Association

Co-Founder & Vice-President

Houston, TX | Sep 2024 - Present

Driven by a desire to promote the unique vibrancy of Hong Kong culture on campus, I co-founded the HKSA to bring students together. As Vice-President, my goal is to build bridges and create an inclusive space where students can celebrate shared heritage and find a sense of belonging at Rice. From organizing nostalgic cultural workshops like egg waffle-making and Cantonese classes to coordinating group Dim Sum trips, I strive to foster connections that make a large university feel like a tight-knit family.

Fencing Team

Epee Player & Mentor

Houston, TX | Sep 2023 - Present

After a hiatus since high school, I returned to the strip at Rice and found something even better than competition: a community. As one of the few experienced fencers on the team, I stepped into a mentorship role, teaching beginners the fundamentals of footwork, blade work, and the complex rules of the sport. Guiding new teammates not only sharpened my own skills but also reignited my love for fencing. I am incredibly grateful for the friends I’ve made here. They are the reason I picked the epee back up, turning a solitary sport into a shared journey.
